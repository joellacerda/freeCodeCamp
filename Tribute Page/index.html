Time line of Lovelace's life

  • 1815 - Ada Lovelace was born on December 10, 1815. Her birth name was Augusta Ada Byron.
  • 1820 - Showed a gift for mathematics at an early age. Her dad, Lord Byron who was a famous poet died when she was only 8 years old. She also had a talent for numbers and language.
  • 1828 - When Ada was only twelve she made a small flying machine.
  • 1833 - While at a party in England Ada first meant Charles Babbage. She was fascinated by Babbage's ideas for a machine that could calculate numbers and presumably write letters. Ada was 17 when she meant Charles Babbage.
  • 1835 - Married William King.
  • 1837 - Ada and Babbage tested the first computer program. And Ada wrote the first computer program.
  • 1838 - William king and Ada Lovelace became Earl and Countess of Lovelace.
  • 1840 - Had a bad habit of gambling every once in a while.
  • 1842 - Translated an Italian mathematician's memoir in french in the subject of the Analytical Engine.
  • 1843 - Published her work and used the initials A.A.L standing for Augusta Ada Lovelace.
  • 1852 - On November 27, 1852 she died at the age of 37. She died because she had cancer. Ada Lovelace got a reward for being the first computer programmer.
